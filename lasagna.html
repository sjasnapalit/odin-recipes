Ingredients for Homemade Lasagna Lasagna noodles – tender pasta that creates the signature layered structure Ground beef – adds savoriness and body to the sauce Olive oil – used for sauteing and adds subtle aroma Onion – provides sweetness that balances the acidity of tomatoes Garlic – adds an aromatic base that enhances the flavor of the meat sauce Crushed tomatoes – create a thick and flavorful sauce base Tomato sauce – smooths the texture and enriches the flavor Chicken broth – keeps the sauce moist and enhances umami Tomato paste – deepens the color and flavor of the sauce Basil – brings freshness and brightness to the sauce Parsley – adds color and balances the rich flavors Italian seasoning – a blend of herbs that ties everything together Salt and ground black pepper – essential for balance Ricotta cheese – gives a creamy layer that contrasts with the tomato base Egg – binds the ricotta mixture Mozzarella cheese – melts beautifully and adds a stretchy cheese texture Parmesan cheese – gives a nutty and slightly salty finish How to Cook Homemade Lasagna Making this homemade lasagna is simpler than it looks. Just take it one step at a time and you will have a beautiful pan ready for your family. Soak the Noodles Step 1 - Soak the noodles Place the lasagna noodles in a large container and cover them with hot water. Let them sit for about 25 minutes until they soften and bend easily. Drain the water and lay the noodles flat. Set them aside while you work on the sauce. I like to start the noodles first so they are ready by the time the sauce finishes simmering. Brown the Beef Step 2 - Brown the beef Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium high heat. Add the ground beef and cook until no pink remains. Break the meat apart with a spoon as it cooks. Let it get a nice color before adding anything else. Saute the Aromatics Step 3 - Saute the aromatics Add the minced garlic and chopped onion to the pot. Cook until the onion turns soft and smells wonderful. You will notice the kitchen starts to smell really good at this point. Build the Tomato Sauce Step 4 - Build the tomato sauce Pour in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, chicken broth, and Italian seasoning. Stir everything together and let it simmer for 20 minutes. Add the tomato paste, chopped basil, and half of the parsley. Keep simmering for another 10 minutes while stirring now and then. I find that this slow simmer is what makes the sauce taste rich and balanced. Rushing it leaves the tomatoes tasting sharp. Season the Sauce Step 5 - Season the sauce Add salt and ground black pepper to taste. Stir in two tablespoons of Parmesan cheese. Turn off the heat and set the pot aside while you prepare the ricotta. Taste the sauce before you move on. This is your chance to adjust the seasoning. Mix the Ricotta Step 6 - Mix the ricotta Combine the ricotta cheese and egg in a bowl. Stir until smooth and creamy. This only takes a minute and makes the ricotta easier to spread between the layers. Assemble the Layers Step 7 - Assemble the layers Preheat your oven to 350 F. Spread a small amount of meat sauce on the bottom of your baking pan. Lay noodles over the sauce, then add more meat sauce, dollops of ricotta, and a good handful of mozzarella and Parmesan. Repeat this layering one more time so you have two full layers. I always make sure each layer gets enough sauce so the noodles stay moist while baking. Finish and Bake Step 8 - Finish and Bake Arrange the last layer of noodles on top. Cover with the remaining meat sauce. Sprinkle the rest of the parsley, mozzarella, and Parmesan generously over the top. Bake for 30 minutes or until the cheese melts and the edges start to bubble. If the top browns too fast, cover the pan loosely with foil for the first half of baking. Rest Before Serving Step 9 - Rest before serving Take the lasagna out of the oven. Let it rest for about 10 minutes before you slice into it. I know it is tempting to cut right away, but waiting helps the layers hold together when you serve. Home
